# PRD — Build to Understand

## Product Overview

**Build to Understand** is a personal creator-led project built around interactive educational longreads and short games.

The core idea is simple:

Take a complex idea from a book, lecture, philosophy concept, scientific topic, or social question — and turn it into a small interactive experience that helps people understand it through play.

Instead of reading another long article, users spend 10–20 minutes exploring an idea through decisions, interaction, and narrative.

The project is positioned as:

**Learn something meaningful in the time people usually spend doomscrolling.**

The platform acts as:

1. A home for all existing and future interactive projects
2. A simple email subscription hub for people who want to receive new releases
3. A long-term audience-building system for future Patreon monetization

---

# Primary Goal

## Main Objective

Build an owned email audience around recurring educational games.

The main KPI is:

**Email subscriptions**

Secondary goals:

* strengthen personal creator brand
* create a recognizable ecosystem around educational games
* build long-term monetization potential via Patreon
* increase reach of existing projects

---

# Success Criteria (First 6–12 Months)

## Success looks like:

* consistent release rhythm of new projects
* growing email list of engaged subscribers
* returning visitors who follow new releases
* clear identity of the project as a recognizable creator platform
* foundation for Patreon launch later

---

# Target Audience

## Primary Audience

Curious people who enjoy learning through engaging formats.

Including:

* students
* intellectually curious general audience
* people who enjoy educational YouTube / essays / explainers
* people interested in philosophy, science, psychology, ethics, history
* readers who like books such as *Sapiens*, *Guns, Germs, and Steel*, moral philosophy explainers, neuroscience content, etc.

## Audience Characteristics

They:

* enjoy learning but dislike dry academic formats
* respond well to interactivity
* appreciate calm, intelligent presentation
* are likely to subscribe for future projects if the first experience feels meaningful

# Product Positioning

## Core Message

I make interactive educational games that help people understand complex ideas.

Each project takes a concept from a book, lecture, or theory and turns it into a playable experience.

Short enough to finish in one sitting.
Meaningful enough to stay with you.

* calm
* intellectual

# Product Structure

## Single-Page Website

The website should be intentionally simple.

Only **one main landing page**.

The goal is clarity and focus:

* understand what the project is
* explore existing games
* subscribe for future releases

No complex navigation.
No content overload.
No distractions.

---

# Core Sections

## 1. Hero Section

### Purpose

Immediately explain what the project is.

Users should understand within 10 seconds:

* what this is
* why it matters
* what they should do next

### Content

Includes:

* short positioning statement
* explanation of the format
* creator framing (personal project)
* primary CTA: **Explore Projects**
* secondary CTA: email subscription

### Emotional Goal

User should feel:

**“This is interesting. I want to try one.”**

---

## 2. Projects Section

### Purpose

Show all existing interactive games.

### Current Projects

1. The Choice
2. Evolution of Civilizations
3. The Brain
4. Reasonable Doubt

### Card Structure

Each project card includes:

* cover image
* title
* short description
* optional tags/topics
* Play CTA

Examples of tags:

* Ethics
* Civilization
* Psychology
* Justice
* Philosophy
* Decision Making

### Priority

Fast access to playing.

Minimal friction.

---

## 3. Subscription Section

### Purpose

Convert visitors into email subscribers.

### Positioning

Soft, honest, lightly ironic.

Example tone:

“If you’d like not to miss the next one.”

or

“Probably better than doomscrolling.”

### Offer

Subscribers receive:

* notification when a new game is released
* short explanation of the new topic
* welcome email with all existing games

No lead magnet.
No fake urgency.
No heavy sales.

Just honest subscription for future projects.

### Form

Simple embedded email form directly on site.

---

## 4. About / Creator Block

### Purpose

Explain briefly who is behind the project.

Not a full personal page.

Short version only:

* who you are
* why you build these projects
* why interactive learning matters

This reinforces trust without shifting focus away from the projects.

---

## 5. External Writing

Long-form essays and additional writing stay on existing Substack.

The site should include:

**Link to Substack**

Not a separate internal blog.

This keeps the product focused.

---

# Subscription Flow

## On Signup

User submits email → receives:

### Welcome Email

Contains:

* short thank-you message
* explanation of the project
* links to all current games

### Future Emails

Sent only when a new game is released.

Includes:

* new game announcement
* short explanation of the topic
* link to play

No unnecessary newsletter spam.

Trust is important.

---

# Future Monetization

## Phase 1

Free access to all games.

Focus only on:

* quality
  n- consistency
* audience growth

## Phase 2

Patreon launch after audience reaches meaningful scale.

### Patreon Value

Likely includes:

* support the creator
* behind-the-scenes notes
* development process
* additional context and research
* optional extended essays

Patreon should feel like support, not paywalling education.
